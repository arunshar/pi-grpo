Physics-Informed RL Post-Training

Pi-GRPO

Physics-informed reinforcement-learning post-training for trajectory generation that treats hard physical constraints as a reward floor, so a policy optimized with PPO, DPO, or GRPO improves task quality without learning to produce physically impossible trajectories.

Abstract

RL post-training (PPO, DPO, GRPO) can reward-hack into physically impossible outputs. Pi-GRPO makes the physics a hard reward floor: the reward is a hybrid of the task reward and a term that heavily penalizes kinematic-constraint violations, so the optimizer is pushed into the feasible region first and then improves task quality within it. The same floor plugs into all three optimizers.

Honest scope. The RL training stack (PPO, DPO, GRPO under the hybrid physics reward) is real and runs. The headline result, hard-constraint violation rate driven from about 18% under vanilla DPO to about 0% with physics-augmented DPO (KL bounded across the run), is the workshop paper's reported experimental result at workshop scale, not a large-scale production benchmark.

Validated output

Reproduced on MSI: the physics reward floor catches 100% of speed-violating trajectories vs 0% without it, even against a hacked +10 preference signal (the reward-level mechanism behind the paper's claim). The ~18%->0% hard-constraint-violation reduction is the workshop paper's reported result ("Physics as a Hard Reward Floor", MOSS @ COLM 2026), not re-run at scale here.
